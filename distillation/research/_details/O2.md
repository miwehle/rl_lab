# O2 Details - H1-16 Relative Up Disinhibition

Supports: [[../observations#O2 H1-16 Acts As Relative Up Disinhibition|O2]]

Subject: Micro-Elise #2, `solar_system_lander_10d_elise_stp_student_64x64_20260721T161456Z`.

Method: greedy policy rollouts over `DEFAULT_WORLD_MIX`, `5` seeds per world, `9696` frames total. The intervention sets `H1-16` to zero after the first ReLU and recomputes `H2` and output Q-values for the same observations.

In `10d` mode, the observation vector is the standard LunarLander `8d` observation plus `dv_x` and `dv_y`, computed as velocity deltas between consecutive observations. These deltas expose world dynamics indirectly; they are not pure gravity because engine thrust, contacts, wind, and simulation state also affect them.

`H1-16` dampens all four actions absolutely, including `up`. The ranking effect is relative: `up` is dampened least, so it becomes more competitive. Ablating `H1-16` nearly removes greedy `up` selection in these rollouts.

## Interpretation

`H1-16` is best described as an indirect main-engine enabler rather than a main-engine exciter. It responds to vertical dynamics and related flight-state signals, is more active in heavier worlds, and suppresses many downstream pathways. Since the suppression is weaker for `up` than for the other actions, the final policy can choose `up` much more often, especially on Earth and Venus.

This is the learned-network version of a roundabout mechanism: not "heavy world -> fire main engine", but "heavy-world flight dynamics -> broad damping -> `up` wins the action ranking more often".
